# Formulary Guardrails — Internal Draft

## Non-negotiable holds

Use **No prescription**, **Request more information**, **Needs in-person care**, or **Urgent care / ER guidance** rather than a prescription when any of the following are unresolved:

- Safety-gate item selected.
- Patient location/licensure cannot be confirmed.
- Photos are inadequate for a visual diagnosis.
- Pregnancy, breastfeeding, or trying to conceive affects the likely medication. Pediatric visits are not offered in this prototype.
- Possible biopsy/procedure need: suspicious pigmented lesion, abscess, changing or bleeding growth, ulcerated lesion, or severe pain.
- Possible systemic infection: fever, rapidly spreading redness, severe pain, necrosis, significant swelling, immunocompromise.
- Ocular involvement: eye pain, light sensitivity, vision changes, periocular HSV/rosacea/dermatitis flare.
- Oral/systemic medication requires missing lab/vital/renal/hepatic/interaction data.

## Compounded medication documentation

For each compounded prescription, the provider dashboard should eventually require:

- Diagnosis / indication.
- Why a compounded product is appropriate for this specific patient.
- Active ingredients and strengths.
- Vehicle/base.
- Directions, quantity, refills, and duration/cycle.
- Allergies and pregnancy/lactation status.
- Pharmacy destination and state coverage.
- Patient counseling text.

## Condition-specific reminders

### Acne
- Avoid antibiotic monotherapy.
- Retinoids are pregnancy-sensitive.
- Isotretinoin remains outside this simple pathway.

### Rosacea
- Separate papules/pustules from erythema/flushing and ocular symptoms.
- Eye symptoms route out.
- Avoid chronic facial topical steroid use.

### Pigment / melasma
- Rule out suspicious lesions.
- Retinoid/hydroquinone-style plans are pregnancy-sensitive.
- Photoprotection is mandatory.

### Hair loss
- Pattern hair loss fits telehealth better than scarring, patchy, painful, pustular, or rapidly progressive hair loss.
- Oral minoxidil/finasteride/spironolactone require additional safety review.


### Scabies
- Treat contacts and environment, not just the patient.
- Pregnancy, infants, immunocompromise, and crusted scabies require higher-touch review.

### Genital warts
- Atypical lesions need biopsy/in-person care.
- Internal/cervical/intra-anal lesions need specialist pathways.
- Pregnancy changes medication selection.

### Nail fungus
- Do not use oral antifungal therapy without reasonable diagnostic confirmation and hepatic/interaction review.

### Aphthous ulcers
- Recurrent minor aphthae can be treated symptomatically.
- Large, nonhealing, genital, eye-associated, febrile, adenopathy-associated, or GI-associated ulcers need workup.

## Product/affiliate separation

- OTC and affiliate products should support skin comfort, barrier care, cleansing, moisturization, sun protection, or appearance.
- Do not imply white-label cosmetics treat diseases unless the product is actually compliant as an OTC drug or prescription drug.
- Affiliate recommendations should remain secondary to clinical judgment and should be disclosed near the recommendation.
