# Step 5 — OTC, White-Label, and Affiliate Product Strategy

## Operating model

Earl Is My Cat should use three separate product lanes:

1. **Owned white-label cosmetic basics** — simple products that support daily routines without disease-treatment claims.
2. **Affiliate / third-party OTC products** — categories already regulated as OTC drugs or higher-risk to own at launch.
3. **Prescription options** — never sold as ecommerce products; prescribed only after a completed visit and clinician review.

This structure keeps the brand simple while reducing regulatory risk.

## Phase 1 owned white-label candidates

Launch only a short shelf:

| Candidate | Why it fits | Claim lane |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gentle Cleanser | Useful across almost every routine | Cleanses without stripping |
| Barrier Moisturizer | Core routine product | Hydrates / comfortable daily moisture |
| Oil-Free Moisturizer | Fits acne-prone and oily skin routines | Lightweight hydration |
| Rough-Feeling Skin Body Lotion | Supports KP/body/friction routines | Helps skin feel smoother / less dry |

Avoid making owned products into condition treatments. The owned line should support the clinical plan, not replace it.

## Phase 1 affiliate categories

Use third-party recommendations for categories that are OTC drugs or higher-claim-risk:

- Mineral sunscreen / tinted mineral sunscreen
- Medicated dandruff shampoo
- Benzoyl peroxide or salicylic acid acne washes
- Clinical-strength antiperspirants
- OTC antifungals
- OTC non-genital wart removers
- Topical minoxidil

Each affiliate section should have a nearby disclosure.

## Defer or avoid at launch

Do not launch these as owned products at first:

- Eyelash growth cosmetic serums
- Skin-lightening or bleaching products
- High-claim anti-aging serums
- Antibacterial or antimicrobial cleansers
- Owned supplements

## Condition mapping rule

Every condition page should follow this pattern:

1. Condition education.
2. Visit CTA for diagnosis and treatment.
3. Supportive products framed as routine support only.

Never write “our moisturizer treats rosacea,” “our cleanser treats acne,” or “our body lotion treats KP.”

Use the product mapping in `assets/data/product-catalog.json` as the canonical internal source.
