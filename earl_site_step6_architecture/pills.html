Supplement concepts

Supplements are a later lane.

Supplements may eventually fit the brand, but they should not be a first-launch product category. They require substantiation, careful label review, and claim discipline.

Delayed

Skin supplement concept

A possible later product only after ingredient review, manufacturing review, adverse-event workflow, and claim substantiation. This should not be positioned as treating acne, rosacea, hair loss, pigment, or any skin disease.

Not for launch
See product strategy
Delayed

Sun support concept

A possible later supplement concept, never a replacement for sunscreen, shade, hats, or protective clothing. Avoid any copy implying UV blocking, sunburn prevention, or skin-cancer risk reduction.

Not for launch
See sunscreen category
Supplement rule. Dietary supplement claims require substantiation and, for structure/function claims, the required disclaimer and FDA notification workflow. They cannot claim to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease.
Launch recommendation. Start with cosmetic basics and affiliate sunscreen/OTC categories. Revisit owned supplements only after legal/regulatory review and manufacturing quality review.
