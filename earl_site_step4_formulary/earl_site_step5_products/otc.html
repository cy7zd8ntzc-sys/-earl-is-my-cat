Shop strategy

The shelf, kept short.

A practical product roadmap for supportive skin basics, white-label candidates, and third-party recommendations. Prescription products stay out of the cart and require a dermatologist visit.

Product lanes
Lane 1

Owned white-label basics

Simple cosmetic products that support daily routines: cleanser, moisturizers, and a small number of low-drama supportive formulas.

  • Keep claims cosmetic and appearance-focused.
  • No disease, antimicrobial, acne-treatment, or barrier-repair claims.
  • Best place to build brand trust first.
Lane 2

Affiliate / third-party OTC

Use existing products for categories that are already OTC drugs or harder to own safely at launch.

  • Sunscreen, dandruff shampoo, acne washes, antiperspirants.
  • Disclose affiliate links close to the recommendation.
  • Prefer non-affiliate alternatives where possible.
Lane 3

Prescription options

Prescription treatment is not ecommerce. It happens only after a visit, clinician review, and patient-specific prescription decision.

  • Commercial Rx or compounded Rx depending on clinical need.
  • Not advertised as a cart product.
  • Routed through the provider workflow.
Core rule. Cosmetics support the routine. OTC drugs are handled as compliant third-party recommendations unless intentionally launched under drug rules. Prescriptions require a visit.

Do not launch first

Eyelash growth cosmetics

Keep growth claims inside the prescription workflow. Cosmetic lash-growth claims create unnecessary drug-claim risk.

Skin-lightening products

Defer owned pigment-lightening products. Use photoprotection recommendations and visit-based prescription decisions first.

High-claim anti-aging serums

Avoid collagen, regeneration, wrinkle-removal, lifting, or structure/function language.

Antibacterial cleansers

Do not sell cleansers with antimicrobial or infection-prevention claims unless intentionally launching a compliant drug product.

Owned supplements

Delay until substantiation, label review, DSHEA disclaimer placement, adverse-event workflow, and manufacturer review are complete.

Claim-safe language

Use

  • Supports healthy-looking skin
  • Helps skin feel hydrated
  • Cleanses without stripping
  • Softens the look of roughness
  • For redness-prone skin

Avoid for cosmetics

  • Treats acne / rosacea / eczema
  • Repairs the skin barrier
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Kills bacteria or fungus
  • Regrows hair or lashes
  • Prevents sun damage
