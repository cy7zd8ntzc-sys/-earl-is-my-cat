# Supplement Strategy

## Recommendation

Do not launch owned supplements in the first version of the site.

Supplements can fit the brand later, but they need more infrastructure than simple cosmetic basics:

- Ingredient and dose review
- Manufacturer quality review
- Label review
- Structure/function claim substantiation
- FDA notification process for structure/function claims when applicable
- DSHEA disclaimer placement
- Adverse-event reporting workflow
- FTC advertising substantiation review

## Public-page position

The supplements page now presents supplements as a later concept, not active products.

## Sun supplement warning

No supplement should be marketed as replacing sunscreen, preventing sunburn, preventing skin cancer, or blocking UV.

## Skin supplement warning

Do not claim a supplement treats acne, rosacea, hair loss, hyperpigmentation, inflammation, herpes, fungal infections, HS, or any dermatologic disease.
