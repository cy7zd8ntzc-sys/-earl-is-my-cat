Molluscum contagiosum

A plain-English guide from Dr. Auston Eckert — what those little bumps are, and how they're handled.

What's actually going on

Molluscum contagiosum is a common, harmless viral skin infection — small, firm, dome-shaped bumps, often in kids but in adults too. It's contagious and spreads easily, and while it can clear on its own, that can take many months, so many people choose to treat it.

What tends to help

  • Avoiding scratching and picking (it spreads them)
  • Not sharing towels or baths
  • Covering bumps when practical
  • Targeted treatment when you'd rather not wait it out

What I'd skip

  • Digging the bumps out yourself
  • Sharing personal items
  • Assuming it'll vanish quickly
