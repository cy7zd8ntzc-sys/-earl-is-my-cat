# Claims and Compliance Audit

This repo is a prototype for a cash-pay teledermatology and skincare site. It is not launch-ready legal or regulatory copy.

## Highest-priority rules

1. **Prescription/compounded medications are not ecommerce products.** Keep them behind the visit workflow and describe them as options that may be prescribed only after physician review.
2. **Compounded medications are not FDA-approved.** Patient-facing language should state this where compounded prescriptions are discussed.
3. **OTC/cosmetic products must not be positioned as treating diseases.** Use supportive cosmetic language: hydrates, cleanses, supports the look/feel of skin, reduces appearance. Avoid: treats, cures, prevents, heals, kills bacteria, reduces inflammation, clears acne/rosacea/eczema, repairs barrier.
4. **Affiliate links need clear disclosure near recommendations.** Keep disclosure visible before or next to product lists.
5. **Condition pages need triage limits.** Add or preserve “when online care is not appropriate” language for infections, genital/STI concerns, eye-adjacent issues, severe pain, fever, rapidly spreading rash, immunosuppression, pregnancy-sensitive medications, and suspected cancer.

## Copy edits already applied

Several stronger treatment phrases were softened across the HTML bundle, including language such as “clear them,” “actually treated,” “genuinely controls,” and “fades them.”

## Before launch

- Attorney review of `terms.html`, `privacy.html`, and `consent.html`.
- Regulatory review of all product copy before white-label products go live.
- Pharmacy contract review for compounding, financial structure, data ownership, adverse-event handling, and state coverage.
- HIPAA/security review before collecting PHI through `visit.html`.
